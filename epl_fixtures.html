Premier League 2024/25

Complete Fixtures & Schedule

The 2024/25 Premier League season kicks off on August 16, 2024, with Manchester United hosting Fulham. Featuring 380 matches across 33 weekends, four midweek rounds, and one Bank Holiday Matchweek, the season concludes on May 25, 2025, with all final-day matches kicking off simultaneously.

Early Season Highlights

Matchweek 2 (August 24-25)

Manchester City hosts Ipswich Town, while Brighton welcomes Manchester United. Aston Villa takes on Arsenal in what promises to be an exciting encounter between two ambitious sides.

Matchweek 3 (August 31-September 1)

The fixtures heat up as West Ham faces Manchester City, Arsenal plays Brighton, and the historic rivalry between Manchester United and Liverpool takes center stage—undoubtedly one of the highlights of the early season.

Matchweek 4 (September 14-15)

The North London derby sees Tottenham Hotspur hosting Arsenal in one of football's fiercest rivalries. Liverpool will play Nottingham Forest, while Manchester City meets Brentford.

Matchweek 5 (September 21-22)

West Ham hosts Chelsea in a London derby, Aston Villa takes on Wolves, and Liverpool welcomes AFC Bournemouth to Anfield.

Championship Sunday

The final day of the season on May 25, 2025, will see all matches kicking off simultaneously at 4:00 PM BST. This ensures fairness in the race for the title, European places, and relegation battles, with no team gaining an advantage from knowing results elsewhere.

Season Overview

With newly promoted teams Leicester City, Ipswich Town, and Southampton eager to prove their worth in the top flight, and established powerhouses like Manchester City, Liverpool, Arsenal, and Chelsea vying for supremacy, the 2024/25 Premier League season promises to be one of the most thrilling yet.

The season features:

  • 380 total matches across all 20 teams
  • 33 weekend fixtures
  • 4 midweek fixture rounds
  • 1 Bank Holiday Matchweek
  • Multiple international breaks throughout the season
