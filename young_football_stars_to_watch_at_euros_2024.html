The Top 10 Best Young Stars At The Euros 2024

The UEFA Euro 2024 quarterfinals are set to feature some of the brightest young talents in football, showcasing their skills on one of the biggest stages in the sport. Here are some of the most promising young players to keep an eye on:

1. Jude Bellingham (England)

At just 20 years old, Jude Bellingham has already made a significant impact on the international stage. A recent Champions League winner with Real Madrid, Bellingham's leadership and skill in midfield could be crucial for England as they aim to end their long wait for a major trophy [Euronews] [Sports Mole].

2. Jamal Musiala (Germany)

Germany's versatile 21-year-old star, Jamal Musiala, is a key player for both Bayern Munich and the national team. Known for his ability to play in multiple attacking roles, Musiala is expected to be a standout performer, especially with Germany hosting the tournament [Euronews].

3. Rasmus Højlund (Denmark)

Rasmus Højlund, the 21-year-old striker from Denmark, has been in excellent form, finishing as Denmark's top scorer in the qualifiers. His pace and goal-scoring ability, honed at Manchester United, make him a player to watch as Denmark looks to make a deep run in the tournament [Euronews].

4. Eduardo Camavinga (France)

Eduardo Camavinga, another 21-year-old prodigy, is a crucial part of France's midfield. A Champions League winner with Real Madrid, Camavinga's ability to control the game and break up opposition attacks will be vital for France's success [Sports Mole].

5. Gavi (Spain)

Gavi, at 19, has already established himself as a key player for both Barcelona and Spain. His creativity and vision in midfield are expected to be pivotal for Spain as they navigate the later stages of the competition [Euronews].

6. Lamine Yamal (Spain)

Spain's 16-year-old sensation, Lamine Yamal, has burst onto the scene with impressive performances for Barcelona. His inclusion in the national squad highlights his rapid development and the potential impact he could have in the Euros [Nine].

7. Benjamin Šeško (Slovenia)

Benjamin Šeško, the 21-year-old striker for RB Leipzig, has had a standout season in the Bundesliga. His goal-scoring prowess and physical presence make him a key player for Slovenia's attack [Euronews].

8. Ryan Gravenberch (Netherlands)

Ryan Gravenberch, the 22-year-old midfielder, has shown his talent at clubs like Ajax, Bayern Munich, and Liverpool. His physicality and technical skills will be essential for the Netherlands as they face tough opponents [Euronews].

9. Warren Zaire-Emery (France)

The 18-year-old midfielder Warren Zaire-Emery has made significant strides at Paris Saint-Germain. His performances have earned him a spot in France's squad, where he is expected to make a notable impact [Nine].

10. Joao Neves (Portugal)

Joao Neves, the 19-year-old from Benfica, has quickly become a key player for Portugal. His ability to control the midfield and provide defensive stability makes him a crucial part of Portugal's plans [Sports Mole].

These young stars are poised to leave a lasting impression at the Euro 2024 quarterfinals, potentially shaping the future of European football with their performances.
