Liverpool vs West Ham: The Tactical Battle That Could Define Their Seasons

As Liverpool and West Ham United prepare to meet at Anfield, the tactical battle between Jurgen Klopp and David Moyes could shape both clubs' seasons. Here's what to expect.

J
James Thornton
27 February 2026 · 12 min read
Liverpool and West Ham players battle for possession in a previous meeting at Anfield.

Jurgen Klopp's tactical whiteboard faces its sternest test yet against West Ham United's visiting squad. With Liverpool's Champions League hopes hanging by a thread and West Ham desperate to avoid relegation, this Anfield showdown isn't just about three points—it's a clash of two managers under intense pressure to deliver.

The High-Stakes Context

When these teams met earlier this season, Liverpool's 5-1 thrashing at the London Stadium exposed West Ham's frailties against Klopp's high press. Now, with both clubs in vastly different circumstances, the tactical battle promises to be even more compelling.

Liverpool enters this match having won three of their last five Premier League fixtures, climbing to within striking distance of the top four. West Ham, meanwhile, sits just two points clear of the relegation zone, their survival hopes hanging by a thread. For David Moyes' side, this represents a chance to gain ground on their rivals at the bottom, while for Klopp, it's an opportunity to make a decisive statement in the race for European football.

For Liverpool, a win would all but secure their place in next season's Champions League, while a defeat could open the door for rivals to leapfrog them in the table. For West Ham, victory would provide a much-needed boost in their fight against relegation, while another loss could leave them staring down the barrel of the Championship.

Liverpool's Tactical Evolution

Klopp's Liverpool have undergone a subtle but significant tactical evolution this season. The high-pressing, gegenpressing style that defined their early years has given way to a more measured, possession-based approach, particularly in the absence of key midfielders like Thiago Alcantara and Naby Keita.

The Reds have increasingly relied on the creativity of Mohamed Salah and Diogo Jota, while the defensive solidity of Ibrahima Konate and Virgil van Dijk has provided a platform for their attacking play. In midfield, the emergence of Curtis Jones as a box-to-box force has added a new dimension to Liverpool's play, allowing them to control games against lesser opponents.

Against West Ham, Klopp is likely to deploy a 4-3-3 formation, with Fabinho anchoring the midfield and allowing Jones and Jordan Henderson to push forward. The full-backs, Andrew Robertson and Trent Alexander-Arnold, will be key in both attack and defense, while the front three of Salah, Jota, and Luis Diaz will look to exploit the spaces left by West Ham's high line.

West Ham's Defensive Dilemma

David Moyes has faced criticism for his side's defensive frailties this season, with West Ham conceding 50 goals in the Premier League—more than any other team in the top half. Against Liverpool's potent attack, Moyes will need to find a way to shore up his defense without stifling his side's attacking threat.

The Hammers' best hope may lie in a compact defensive shape, with Declan Rice and Tomas Soucek screening the back four and preventing Liverpool's midfielders from dictating the tempo. In attack, Moyes is likely to rely on the pace and creativity of Jarrod Bowen and Said Benrahma, who will look to exploit the spaces left by Liverpool's high line.

However, with Liverpool's full-backs pushing high and wide, West Ham will need to be wary of the counter-attacking threat posed by Salah and Jota. Moyes may opt for a more conservative approach, with the Hammers looking to absorb pressure and hit Liverpool on the break.

The Battle in Midfield

The midfield battle will be crucial in determining the outcome of this match. Liverpool's Fabinho will be tasked with controlling the tempo and breaking up West Ham's attacks, while Henderson and Jones will look to dictate play in the final third.

For West Ham, Declan Rice will be key in breaking up Liverpool's attacks and providing a defensive shield for his backline. Soucek, meanwhile, will look to support Rice in midfield and provide an outlet for his side's attacks. The battle between these two midfield units will be pivotal in determining the outcome of the match.

Liverpool's midfield trio will need to be at their best to control the game and prevent West Ham from gaining a foothold. If Fabinho, Henderson, and Jones can dominate the midfield battle, Liverpool will have a clear route to victory. However, if West Ham can assert themselves in the middle of the park, they may be able to create enough chances to trouble the Reds' defense.

The Impact of Key Players

For Liverpool, Mohamed Salah will once again be the man to watch. The Egyptian has been in scintillating form this season, with 17 goals and 9 assists in the Premier League. His ability to create and score from nothing will be crucial in breaking down West Ham's defense.

Diogo Jota, meanwhile, has emerged as a key player for Liverpool this season, with 12 goals and 6 assists in the league. His pace and movement will be vital in stretching West Ham's defense and creating spaces for Salah and Luis Diaz to exploit.

For West Ham, Jarrod Bowen has been a standout performer this season, with 10 goals and 5 assists in the Premier League. His pace and creativity will be crucial in testing Liverpool's defense, while Said Benrahma's ability to take on defenders will be key in creating chances for his side.

The battle between these key players will be crucial in determining the outcome of the match. If Salah and Jota can find a way to break down West Ham's defense, Liverpool will have a clear route to victory. However, if Bowen and Benrahma can trouble the Reds' backline, West Ham may be able to create enough chances to secure a positive result.

Looking Ahead

As the season reaches its climax, every match takes on added significance. For Liverpool, this clash with West Ham represents a chance to solidify their position in the top four and take a step closer to Champions League qualification. For West Ham, it's an opportunity to gain ground on their rivals at the bottom and secure their Premier League status for another season.

The tactical battle between Klopp and Moyes promises to be one of the most compelling of the season, with both managers looking to outwit each other and secure a vital three points. As the teams prepare to face off at Anfield, the stage is set for a high-stakes showdown that could shape the destiny of both clubs.

In the end, the team that can impose their tactical vision on the match will likely emerge victorious. For Liverpool, that means controlling the midfield and exploiting the spaces left by West Ham's high line. For West Ham, it means staying compact defensively and hitting Liverpool on the counter-attack. Whichever team can execute their game plan to perfection will have the upper hand in this crucial clash.
