Late Sesko Goal Gives Man United Dramatic Win Over Fulham in Five-Goal Thriller

A dramatic late goal from Benjamin Sesko secured a crucial three points for Manchester United in a pulsating encounter at Old Trafford.

By James Hartley
1 February 2026 · 10 min read

Benjamin Sesko’s left foot etched his name into Manchester United folklore on a night that will be replayed for years to come. In a match that had everything—drama, controversy, and sheer entertainment—the 22-year-old Slovenian striker struck in the 94th minute to cap a 3-2 victory over Fulham that felt like a statement of intent from Michael Carrick’s side.

Old Trafford erupted as Sesko’s strike found the top corner, sealing United’s third straight Premier League win and sending Fulham home with a sense of what might have been. This was a game that swung wildly from one end to the other, a microcosm of the season so far for both teams, and a reminder of why football remains the most unpredictable of sports.

The Match: A Rollercoaster of Emotions

The first half was a cagey affair, with both sides feeling each other out. Fulham, under Marco Silva, have been known for their defensive solidity this season, but they were caught out early when United’s pressing forced a mistake deep in their own half. The ball fell to Alejandro Garnacho, who played a delightful through ball to Rasmus Højlund, who slotted past Bernd Leno in the Fulham goal to give United an early lead.

Fulham responded well, though, and equalized just before halftime through André-Frank Zambo Anguissa, who capitalized on a defensive error to fire past André Onana. The second half saw both teams push for a winner, and it was Fulham who took the lead through Carlos Vinícius, who latched onto a through ball and rounded Onana to make it 2-1.

United, though, are made of sterner stuff this season. They equalized through a well-worked team goal finished by Bruno Fernandes, setting the stage for Sesko’s heroics. The Slovenian, who has been a revelation since his summer move, latched onto a loose ball in the box and fired home in the 94th minute, sending the home fans into raptures and condemning Fulham to a heartbreaking defeat.

Tactical Analysis: Carrick’s United Find Their Groove

Michael Carrick has been under pressure to deliver results, and this win will do wonders for his credibility. His decision to play a high pressing game paid dividends, as United’s front three of Garnacho, Højlund, and Fernandes harried Fulham into mistakes. The wingers provided width, while Fernandes orchestrated play from deep, showing why he remains one of the Premier League’s most influential players.

Fulham, meanwhile, looked dangerous on the counter but struggled to break down United’s defensive shape. Silva’s decision to play a back three was a tactical gamble that didn’t pay off, as United’s wingers exploited the space left behind. The Cottagers will rue their defensive lapses, particularly in the lead-up to Sesko’s winner.

United’s buildup play was a joy to watch, with the midfield trio of Kobbie Mainoo, Christian Eriksen, and Fernandes controlling the tempo. Their ability to switch play and find runners in behind was key, and it was no surprise that their goals came from quick transitions.

Key Performers: Sesko Shines, Fernandes Delivers

Benjamin Sesko was the undoubted star of the show. His goal was a moment of pure quality, but his overall performance was one of relentless running and intelligent movement. The Slovenian has been a revelation since his move from RB Leipzig, and this goal will only enhance his growing reputation.

Bruno Fernandes was once again at the heart of everything for United. His goal was crucial, but his all-round play—pressing, passing, and creating chances—was exemplary. The Portuguese midfielder has been in fine form this season, and this performance will do his chances of a new contract no harm.

For Fulham, Vinícius was a constant threat. His goal was a fine finish, and he caused United’s defense problems all night. However, his teammates will need to step up if they are to challenge for a European spot this season.

The Manager’s Perspective: Carrick’s Masterstroke

Michael Carrick’s decision to stick with his high-pressing game was a masterstroke. His team selection and tactical tweaks paid off, and his post-match comments were filled with pride. Carrick’s ability to manage the pressure and keep his players focused will be key in the coming months. This win will give him a boost, but he knows there is still much work to do. The manager’s tactical acumen was on full display, and his ability to make in-game adjustments was crucial in turning the tide.

What This Means for the Table

This win moves Manchester United up the table, and their third straight victory is a sign of growing momentum. With key players returning from injury and a clear tactical plan in place, United are starting to look like a team capable of challenging for a top-four spot.

For Fulham, this defeat is a setback in their push for European football. They have shown glimpses of quality this season, but their defensive frailties have let them down. Silva will need to address these issues if his side is to mount a serious challenge.

The Premier League table is tightening, and this result could have a significant impact on the final standings. United’s win keeps them in the hunt, while Fulham’s defeat is a reminder of the fine margins that separate success from disappointment in the top flight.

Looking Ahead: The Road to Europe

For Manchester United, the focus now turns to their next Premier League fixture. The momentum they have built up is crucial, and they will be looking to continue their winning run. With key players returning to fitness and a clear tactical plan in place, United are starting to look like a team capable of challenging for a top-four spot.

Fulham, meanwhile, will need to regroup quickly. Their push for European football is far from over, but they will need to address their defensive issues if they are to mount a serious challenge. Silva’s side has shown they can compete with the best, but consistency has been a problem, and they will need to find a way to string together a run of positive results.

This match was a reminder of why football is so unpredictable. One moment can change everything, and Sesko’s goal will be remembered for years to come. For United, it was a statement of intent. For Fulham, it was a harsh lesson in the fine margins that separate success from disappointment in the Premier League.
