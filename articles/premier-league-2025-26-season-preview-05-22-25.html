2. New Rules and Technology

The 2025/26 season introduces changes to enhance gameplay and fairness. A new rule, approved by Premier League clubs, allows only team captains to speak to referees during matches, aiming to reduce on-field disputes. Additionally, semi-automated offside technology, trialed successfully in the FA Cup, will be fully implemented to improve decision accuracy.

3. Promoted Teams and Stadium Changes

After intense EFL play-offs, Leeds United, Burnley, and the winner of the Championship play-off final between Sheffield United and Sunderland (set for May 24, 2025) will join the Premier League, replacing relegated Ipswich Town, Leicester City, and Southampton.

  • Leeds United: Back after a brief absence, they aim to re-establish themselves in the top flight.
  • Burnley: Known for their resilience, Burnley return with a fresh kit design featuring thicker stripes.
  • Everton’s New Home: Everton will debut their Hill Dickinson Stadium, leaving Goodison Park after over a century.
