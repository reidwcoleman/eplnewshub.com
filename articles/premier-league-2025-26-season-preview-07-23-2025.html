Premier League 2025/26 Season Preview

By EPL News Hub | July 23, 2025

As the 2025/26 Premier League season approaches, kicking off on August 15 with champions Liverpool hosting Bournemouth, football fans are buzzing with anticipation. Following Liverpool's dominant 2024/25 campaign where they clinched their second Premier League title in the Arne Slot era and their 20th English championship overall, the stage is set for another thrilling year. With promoted sides Leeds United, Burnley, and Sunderland joining the fray after securing promotion from the Championship, and significant summer transfers reshaping squads across the division, this preview delves into the key storylines, from title defenses to relegation scraps. For more on last season's action, check our latest EPL standings, and stay tuned for detailed fixture analysis in our upcoming pieces.

Title Contenders and Favorites

Liverpool, under Arne Slot, enter as defending champions and clear favorites, bolstered by marquee signings like Florian Wirtz from Bayer Leverkusen for £100m, Milos Kerkez from Bournemouth for £40m, and Giorgi Mamardashvili as a new goalkeeper option. Their squad depth and tactical prowess make them the team to beat, with experts giving them a high probability of retaining the title. Arsenal, who finished second last season with 74 points but showed resilience, remain strong challengers after a solid transfer window, aiming to bridge the gap with strategic additions.

Manchester City, despite a quieter window compared to rivals, added talents like Rayan Ait-Nouri and Rayan Cherki, but face questions over aging stars like Kevin De Bruyne. Predictions from experts and AI models suggest a tight race among these three giants, with Liverpool tipped to defend their title successfully. Chelsea and Manchester United, with new additions like João Pedro for Chelsea (£60m) and Bryan Mbeumo for United (£70m), could disrupt the top order if they gel quickly under their respective managers. Newcastle United and Aston Villa are also dark horses, eyeing top-four finishes after strong previous campaigns.

Promoted Teams and Relegation Battles

The newly promoted trio of Leeds United, Burnley, and Sunderland bring fresh energy and historical rivalries back to the league. Leeds, as automatic promotion winners under Daniel Farke, aim to re-establish themselves as a top-flight mainstay with a blend of youth and experience. Burnley, returning after a brief stint in the Championship, and playoff victors Sunderland add intrigue, including the much-anticipated return of the Tyne-Wear derby against Newcastle. These teams replace the relegated sides from last season: Leicester City, Ipswich Town, and Southampton, who struggled to adapt and were sent down after tough campaigns.

Early predictions point to the promoted teams facing tough survival fights, alongside perennial strugglers like Bournemouth, Fulham, and perhaps Everton, who continue to battle financial constraints. However, surprises are always possible in the Premier League's unforgiving environment, where tactical acumen and squad cohesion can defy odds. Pundits warn that without smart recruitment, these sides could follow the fate of last year's promoted teams, all of whom were relegated.

Key Transfers and Managerial Changes

This summer's transfer window has been a blockbuster, with over £1 billion spent across the league, reflecting the growing financial power of English football. Liverpool's acquisitions of Wirtz, Kerkez, and Hugo Ekitike (£69m) stand out as statements of intent, while Chelsea splashed on João Pedro (£60m) and other talents to bolster their attack. Manchester City secured Ait-Nouri (£31m) for defensive reinforcement, and Aston Villa added depth with young prospects.

On the managerial front, Manchester United appointed Ruben Amorim after sacking Erik ten Hag mid-season last year, hoping his tactical nous revives the Red Devils. Other notable changes include Vítor Pereira at Wolves, aiming to stabilize the club after a turbulent period. These moves could define the season, with experts noting that big spenders like Liverpool and Chelsea are gearing up for an epic title race, while mid-table teams focus on consolidation.

European Qualification and Mid-Table Ambitions

Beyond the title race, the battle for European spots promises intensity. With expanded Champions League formats, top-four finishes are more crucial than ever. Arsenal, Manchester City, and Liverpool are locks, but Newcastle, Tottenham, and Aston Villa will vie for the remaining berths, potentially joined by resurgent Chelsea or United. Mid-table sides like Brighton, West Ham, and Crystal Palace aim for Europa League or Conference League qualification, leveraging smart recruitment and youth development.

Teams like Brentford and Fulham, known for punching above their weight, could surprise with consistent performances, while Everton and Bournemouth focus on stability amid off-field challenges.

Players to Watch

  • Florian Wirtz (Liverpool): The £100m man from Bayer Leverkusen is expected to dazzle in midfield with his creativity and goal-scoring prowess.
  • Erling Haaland (Manchester City): Continues to be a goal machine, with experts predicting another Golden Boot challenge.
  • Alexander Isak (Newcastle): Tipped for a breakout after last season's impressive form, potentially leading the line for European aspirations.
  • Mikel Merino (Arsenal): A key signing expected to add steel and vision to the Gunners' midfield.
  • Bryan Mbeumo (Manchester United): The £70m arrival from Brentford could provide the spark United need in attack.
