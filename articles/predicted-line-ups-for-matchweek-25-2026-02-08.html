Predicted Line-ups for Matchweek 25

Get the latest predicted line-ups for Matchweek 25 in the Premier League, with insights into team news, injuries, and tactical formations. Who will start and who will miss out?

By Tom Harrison
7 February 2026 · 12 min read

The Premier League is gearing up for another thrilling matchweek, with several high-stakes fixtures on the horizon. As the teams prepare to take to the pitch, the big question on everyone's mind is: who will start and who will miss out? In this article, we'll delve into the predicted line-ups for Matchweek 25, taking into account the latest team news, injuries, and tactical formations.

Manchester City vs. Arsenal: A Clash of the Titans

One of the most highly anticipated matches of the week is the showdown between Manchester City and Arsenal. City are expected to field a strong lineup, with Erling Haaland leading the charge up front. The Norwegian striker has been in phenomenal form, scoring 23 goals in 24 appearances for the Citizens. As Pep Guardiola noted in his pre-match press conference:

"Erling is a special player, and we're lucky to have him. He's been working hard and deserves all the credit for his performances."
Arsenal, on the other hand, will be looking to bounce back from their disappointing defeat to Everton last week. Mikel Arteta is likely to stick with his trusted 4-3-3 formation, with Bukayo Saka and Gabriel Martinelli providing width on the flanks.

Tactical Battles: Liverpool vs. Chelsea

Liverpool and Chelsea are set to engage in a tactical battle, with both teams looking to outmaneuver each other. The Reds are expected to line up in their customary 4-3-3 formation, with Thiago Alcantara and Fabinho anchoring the midfield. As Jurgen Klopp explained:

"We need to be smart and flexible in our approach. Chelsea are a tough opponent, and we'll need to be at our best to get the three points."
Chelsea, meanwhile, will be looking to exploit Liverpool's defensive vulnerabilities, particularly down the flanks. Graham Potter is likely to opt for a 3-4-3 formation, with Reece James and Ben Chilwell providing attacking width.

The Premier League is a league of fine margins, and the difference between success and failure can come down to a single tactical decision.

Injury Concerns: Who Will Miss Out?

Injuries are a major concern for several Premier League teams, with some key players set to miss out on Matchweek 25. Harry Kane is a doubt for Tottenham's clash with Wolves, after picking up a knock in training. As Antonio Conte noted:

"Harry is a crucial player for us, and we'll need to assess his fitness before making a decision."
Manchester United, meanwhile, will be without the services of Christian Eriksen, who is sidelined with a long-term injury. Erik ten Hag will need to find a suitable replacement in midfield, with Donny van de Beek and James Garner potential options.

Conclusion: What to Expect from Matchweek 25

Matchweek 25 promises to be an exciting and unpredictable weekend of Premier League action. With several key fixtures and intriguing team selections, there's plenty to look forward to. As the teams take to the pitch, one thing is certain: only the strongest will survive.

The Premier League is a true test of endurance, and only the teams with the deepest squads and strongest tactics will emerge victorious.
Will your team come out on top, or will they succumb to the pressure? One thing's for sure: we can't wait to find out.
