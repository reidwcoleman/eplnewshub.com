Premier League 2025-26 Table: The Title Race and Relegation Battle Heats Up

With just months remaining, the Premier League title race is as tight as ever, while the relegation battle intensifies. Here's what the latest standings tell us about the season's defining struggles.

James Thornton
20 February 2026 · 12 min read
The Premier League title race is heating up as Arsenal lead Manchester City by five points with just months remaining in the season.

Arsenal’s 2-0 dismantling of Liverpool at Anfield was a brutal reminder of their title credentials. Mikel Arteta’s side now lead Manchester City by five points, with a +32 goal difference that highlights their ruthless efficiency. But this is no time for complacency. City are lurking, and Arsenal must keep delivering.

The Title Race: Arsenal’s Dominance vs. City’s Pursuit

Arsenal’s 17 wins, 7 draws, and 3 losses have them in pole position, but City’s 53 points—just five behind—prove they’re not going away. City’s +30 goal difference is impressive, but their five losses show they’re vulnerable. Arsenal’s attacking trio of Declan Rice, Martin Ødegaard, and Bukayo Saka have been relentless, while City rely on Kevin De Bruyne and Phil Foden. If Arsenal maintain their defensive solidity—32 goals conceded, second-lowest in the league—they’ll hold off City. But if injuries strike, Guardiola’s side will exploit any weakness.

Aston Villa’s Resurgence and the Top Four Scramble

Aston Villa’s +10 goal difference shows their attacking intent, but six losses highlight their inconsistency. Unai Emery’s side sit third on 50 points, but Manchester United’s surge under Michael Carrick—now on 45 points—has turned the Champions League spot into a nail-biter. Chelsea (44 points) and Liverpool (42) aren’t far behind, making the top four a rolling drama.

Tactically, United’s defensive switch under Carrick has paid off, with a +10 goal difference. Chelsea’s +17 goal difference shows their unpredictability, while Liverpool’s +6 highlights their defensive frailties. But with Darwin Núñez and Mohamed Salah, Liverpool always pose a threat.

The Relegation Battle: Who’s Going Down?

Brentford’s 40 points keep them clear, but their +5 goal difference pales next to Arsenal’s +32. Everton, on 37 points with a -1 goal difference, are in the drop zone and need a miracle. Sunderland, Burnley, and Leeds United have shown flashes, but their inconsistency could doom them.

The bottom half is a minefield. Brentford and Everton have the quality to stay up, but their form is waning. Sunderland, Burnley, and Leeds can compete but lack consistency. The next few months will decide who survives.

The Managerial Impact: Carrick’s Masterstroke and Emery’s Revival

Michael Carrick’s appointment at Manchester United has been brilliant. He’s transformed them into a disciplined unit, with a +10 goal difference proving their defensive improvement. If Carrick keeps this up, United will finish in the top four—a stunning turnaround.

Unai Emery has revitalized Aston Villa. His tactical flexibility and ability to maximize players like Ollie Watkins and Emiliano Buendía have made Villa title contenders. If he keeps them in the top four, it’ll be a remarkable first season.

Looking Ahead: The Standings Speak

The Premier League 2025-26 season is delivering drama. Arsenal lead, but City are hunting them. The top four is a mess, with Villa, United, Chelsea, and Liverpool all in the mix. At the bottom, Brentford, Everton, and the promoted sides are fighting for survival. The Premier League never disappoints—expect more twists, turns, and unforgettable moments.
