FA Cup Quarter-Finals 2026: Fixtures, Predictions and Key Battles

Four ties, eight clubs and a place at Wembley on the line. We break down every quarter-final matchup, from Arsenal's blockbuster clash with Manchester City to the London derby between Chelsea and Spurs.

The FA Cup quarter-finals are set. Four ties will determine which clubs reach Wembley in April. Photo: EPL News Hub

The FA Cup quarter-finals have a habit of producing football's most unforgiving theatre — 90 minutes during which reputations built over a season can be confirmed or collapsed in a single afternoon. This year's last-eight draw has delivered exactly the kind of chaos the competition thrives on: no quarter, no consolation prizes, and Wembley waiting at the end of it all. Arsenal host Manchester City in a fixture that barely needs a build-up, Liverpool welcome a Nottingham Forest side that has spent the season cheerfully defying expectation, Chelsea and Tottenham renew one of English football's most combustible rivalries, and Manchester United face a Newcastle side that has won more admirers than they have. Two weekends that will reshape conversations for the rest of the season.

Arsenal vs Manchester City: The Main Event

There was a time when Arsenal versus Manchester City felt like a foregone conclusion. City would press Mikel Arteta's side into a series of nervous errors, the Emirates would sigh collectively, and the tie would be over by the hour mark. That dynamic no longer holds. Arsenal have become a side that meets City's intensity with something resembling contempt — they invite pressure, absorb it, and punish the space that opens up behind a high defensive line.

City arrive at the Emirates with questions still unanswered about their defensive shape following a run of fixtures that exposed Ruben Dias at set pieces. Bukayo Saka, coming off arguably the most consistent month of his career, will probe Kyle Walker at every opportunity on that right flank, while Martin Odegaard in the half-spaces has begun to look genuinely unplayable when given the ball in rhythm. For City, Phil Foden is the key: when he occupies the pocket behind the striker and ghosts between Arsenal's midfield lines, City are a different proposition entirely.

Tactically, this match will be decided by who controls the second phase after City's initial press is beaten. Arsenal's back-four has become supremely comfortable playing through pressure, and if Declan Rice can keep the lid on City's central midfield engine, the home side should have enough to advance. Erling Haaland, criminally absent from recent form, will be a constant aerial threat from corners — Arsenal cannot afford to concede early.

"At the Emirates, against this Arsenal team, City will have to earn everything they get. There will be no freebies here."

Prediction: Arsenal 2–1 Manchester City. Saka with an assist, Odegaard off the bench sealing it late after Haaland briefly levels the tie.

Liverpool vs Nottingham Forest: Anfield's Toughest Test

Nottingham Forest should not be here. That is the conventional wisdom, and it is precisely the kind of thinking that Nuno Espirito Santo has spent the season weaponising. Forest have beaten three Premier League sides en route to the quarter-finals, each time with the same stubborn, energetic block that makes them infuriating to break down. They defend deep, they transition ferociously, and they have Anthony Elanga running at full-backs with the enthusiasm of someone who has not read the scouting report telling him he is the underdog.

Liverpool, for all their quality, have looked vulnerable when opponents sit in a low block and dare them to unlock it laterally. Mohamed Salah, now operating in more of a free role on the right, can exploit Forest's tendency to commit their left-back forward late in attacks. Luis Diaz has been the form player of Liverpool's season — creative, direct, and with an eye for goal that makes him the most dangerous man in this tie. The key for Anfield's side will be patience. Forest will wait. They will absorb. And they will hit on the counter with the speed and directness of a side that has nothing to lose.

Nottingham Forest have already beaten three top-flight sides to reach this stage. Anfield will be the hardest test yet — but Forest have never cared much for reputation.

Forest's best hope is an early goal that forces Liverpool out of their shape, opening the kind of central corridors that Callum Hudson-Odoi thrives in. It is a legitimate game plan, but Anfield in a cup tie is one of the most hostile environments in European football. Liverpool's home record in knockout football remains extraordinary.

Prediction: Liverpool 2–0 Nottingham Forest. Controlled, professional, clinical. Diaz opens the scoring before the half-hour; Salah adds gloss late on.

Chelsea vs Tottenham: London's Most Combustible Fixture

The London derby in a cup quarter-final generates an atmosphere that most clubs will never experience in their lifetimes — and this edition arrives at a peculiar moment for both clubs. Chelsea under Enzo Maresca have found a genuine playing identity, pressing with purpose and rotating a squad depth that remains the envy of the division. Tottenham, resurgent under their current structure, have been playing with a directness that makes them dangerous in exactly the kind of open, end-to-end game this fixture often becomes.

Cole Palmer has been the decisive figure in Chelsea's campaign — technically brilliant, with an ability to find solutions in tight spaces that few players in European football can match. His partnership with Nicolas Jackson has grown from tentative to genuinely threatening over the course of the season. For Spurs, Son Heung-min remains the creative heartbeat, ageing beautifully and still capable of the individual moment that changes a game. Dominic Solanke, reunited with Postecoglou's system, brings the aerial and link-up quality that Tottenham's attacking play often needs when Palmer's intelligence is being nullified.

Chelsea's pressing intensity can be disrupted by Spurs' quick vertical passing — Tottenham's fullbacks are instructed to get forward early and late, and their timing often catches high defensive lines exposed. This is the most evenly matched tie of the round.

Cole Palmer versus Son Heung-min, Stamford Bridge, FA Cup quarter-final. You could not design a better advertisement for this competition.

Prediction: Chelsea 1–1 Tottenham Hotspur after 90 minutes, Chelsea advance on penalties. The shootout is where Stamford Bridge's atmosphere becomes a 12th man. Spurs' nerve will go first.

Manchester United vs Newcastle: Old Trafford Needs This

Manchester United's relationship with the FA Cup is the last remaining strand connecting this club to its recent sense of self. Two finals in three years have delivered one trophy and one near-miss, and the competition has become, perhaps unfairly, the primary benchmark by which this squad's character is judged. Against Newcastle, United will need every scrap of that experience, because Eddie Howe's side arrive as genuine contenders rather than respectful opposition.

Newcastle's engine room is one of the most industrious in the division. Bruno Guimaraes controls tempo with the authority of a player who belongs in a Champions League semi-final, while Alexander Isak — sharp, clever, devastatingly quick in behind — gives United's centre-backs problems they will have spent the week specifically preparing for. For United, the story is Marcus Rashford, who has rediscovered a version of himself that Old Trafford has been waiting years to see: direct, decisive, and willing to run at defenders rather than away from them.

The tactical wrinkle here is Newcastle's width. Howe instructs his fullbacks to overlap aggressively, which stretches United's defensive shape and creates dangerous overloads. United's midfield will need to track those runners late, something they have not always been disciplined enough to manage. Kobbie Mainoo, arguably United's most important player in this fixture, must dominate the space in front of the back four.

Old Trafford, when it is fully invested, still generates a noise and an expectation that weighs on visiting sides. Newcastle are experienced enough not to be rattled by it — but United's home advantage, combined with the cup pedigree in their squad, tips the balance fractionally.

Prediction: Manchester United 2–1 Newcastle United. A goal from Rashford just after the hour proves the decisive moment. Isak pulls one back, but United hold. The Wembley dream survives another round.
