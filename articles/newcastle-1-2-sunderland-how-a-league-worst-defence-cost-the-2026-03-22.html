That was until the 90th minute, when Sunderland struck the winner, sending their fans into raptures and leaving the Newcastle players stunned.

Eddie Howe, the Newcastle manager, was visibly distraught after the game, admitting that the defeat was "very, very painful". So, what went wrong for Newcastle? The answer lies in their defensive frailties, which have been a recurring theme throughout the season. With 22 points dropped from winning positions in the Premier League, Newcastle's inability to see out games has been a major concern. The Black Cats' winner, in particular, was a testament to Newcastle's lax defending, as they failed to deal with a straightforward attack.

The statistics make for grim reading, with Newcastle's winless run in the league against Sunderland now stretching to 11 games. This dismal record is a stark reminder of the team's struggles against their local rivals, and it is an issue that Howe will need to address if he is to revive their title hopes. For now, though, the focus will be on picking up the pieces and regrouping for the next challenge.

The Manager's View
