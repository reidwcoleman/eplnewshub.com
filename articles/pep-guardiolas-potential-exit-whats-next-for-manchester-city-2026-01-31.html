Pep Guardiola's Potential Exit: What's Next for Manchester City?

✍️EPL News Hub
📅January 30, 2026
⏱️10 min read
Pep Guardiola's Manchester City future in doubt
The Spanish manager's potential departure has left fans and pundits wondering what's next for the club

The future of Manchester City manager Pep Guardiola has been the subject of intense speculation in recent weeks, with growing conviction among Premier League managers that the Spanish tactician will leave the Etihad Stadium this summer. As the news continues to unfold, City fans and pundits alike are left to ponder the implications of Guardiola's potential exit and what it could mean for the club's future.

The Guardiola Era: A Legacy of Success

Pep Guardiola's tenure at Manchester City has been nothing short of remarkable. Since his arrival in 2016, the club has experienced unprecedented success, winning four Premier League titles, two FA Cups, and two League Cups. The Spanish manager's innovative tactics and meticulous attention to detail have transformed the team into a dominant force in English football.

Guardiola's commitment to developing young players and improving existing talent has been a hallmark of his regime. The likes of Phil Foden, Jack Grealish, and Ruben Dias have all flourished under his guidance, becoming integral components of the City squad. As Guardiola himself noted in a recent interview, 'the most important thing for me is to help the players improve and become better versions of themselves'.

Implications of Guardiola's Potential Exit

The potential departure of Pep Guardiola would undoubtedly have significant consequences for Manchester City. The club's playing style, which has been so heavily influenced by the Spanish manager's philosophy, may undergo a significant transformation. As former City player and current pundit, Micah Richards, observed, 'Guardiola's exit would be a huge loss for the club, but it could also provide an opportunity for a new manager to come in and put their own stamp on the team'.

The impact on the team's recruitment strategy could also be substantial. Guardiola's influence on transfer dealings has been well-documented, and his departure may lead to a shift in the club's approach to signing new players. According to reports, City's hierarchy is already preparing for life after Guardiola, with a shortlist of potential replacements reportedly being compiled.

Potential Replacements for Guardiola

With Guardiola's future uncertain, speculation has begun to mount regarding potential replacements. Borussia Dortmund's Marco Rose has been mooted as a possible candidate, given his experience in the Champions League and his ability to develop young players. Former Tottenham manager, Mauricio Pochettino, is another name that has been linked to the vacancy, with his Premier League experience and attractive brand of football making him an attractive option.

However, former Barcelona manager, Xavi Hernandez, is also rumored to be in the running, having impressed in his maiden managerial stint with Al Sadd in Qatar. The Spanish tactician's knowledge of the Barcelona academy, where Guardiola cut his teeth, could prove invaluable in attracting top talent to the Etihad Stadium.

A New Era for Manchester City?

While the prospect of Pep Guardiola's departure may be daunting for Manchester City fans, it also presents an opportunity for the club to embark on a new era of success. The City hierarchy has consistently demonstrated its ability to attract and retain top talent, both on and off the pitch, and there is no reason to suggest that this will change in the post-Guardiola era.

As City's director of football, Txiki Begiristain, noted in a recent interview, 'the club is always planning for the future, and we are confident that we have the structures in place to ensure continued success, regardless of who is in the dugout'.

While the uncertainty surrounding Guardiola's future may be unsettling for City fans, one thing is clear: the club will continue to be a major force in English football, with or without the Spanish manager at the helm.
