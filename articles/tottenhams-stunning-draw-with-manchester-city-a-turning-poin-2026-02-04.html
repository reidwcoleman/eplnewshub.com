Tottenham's Stunning Draw with Manchester City: A Turning Point in the Title Race?

A stunning draw between Tottenham and Manchester City has thrown the title race wide open, as Manchester United's dramatic win adds to the excitement. Can Spurs and United capitalize on City's slip-up?

By Emily Wilson
3 February 2026 · 15 min read

The Premier League title race has been turned on its head after a dramatic weekend of football. Tottenham's stunning comeback against Manchester City has left the door ajar for Manchester United, who took full advantage with a thrilling win of their own. As the season enters its final stretch, the question on everyone's lips is: can Spurs and United capitalize on City's slip-up to mount a serious title challenge?

The Dramatic Draw

It was a match that will be remembered for a long time, as Tottenham fought back from 2-0 down to earn a point against the defending champions. Manchester City, known for their ruthless efficiency, were caught off guard by Spurs' relentless pressure in the second half.

"We showed great character and spirit to come back from 2-0 down,"
said Tottenham manager Antonio Conte, beaming with pride after the match.

The stats tell the story of a City dominance that was slowly but surely eroded by Tottenham's persistence. City enjoyed 62% possession and created 2.5 expected goals (xG) in the first half, but Spurs turned the tables in the second, creating 1.8 xG and limiting City to just 0.5 xG. It was a testament to Conte's tactical acumen and his team's willingness to adapt and overcome.

"I think we deserved the point, and maybe even the three points," said Harry Kane, who scored the equalizer for Tottenham. "We showed great desire and passion, and that's what we need to take into the rest of the season." (Source: BBC Sport)

Title Implications

The draw has significant implications for the title race, as Manchester City's lead at the top has been trimmed to just three points. Manchester United, who won 3-2 against a resilient West Ham, are now firmly in the hunt, with Spurs still clinging to the coattails of the top two. The question is, can either team sustain a challenge over the remaining matches?

Historically, City have been known to falter in the final stretch of the season, and this draw has given their rivals hope.

"We're not out of it yet,"
said Manchester United manager Erik ten Hag, his eyes fixed on the prize. "We need to keep winning and see where it takes us."

The run-in will be crucial, with City facing tricky matches against Liverpool and Arsenal, while United and Spurs have more manageable fixtures on paper. However, as we've seen time and time again in the Premier League, nothing is certain, and the title race is far from over.

Conte's Tactical Masterclass

Antonio Conte's tactics were instrumental in Tottenham's comeback, as he switched to a 3-4-3 formation in the second half and introduced fresh legs to harass City's defense. The Italian's decision to push his wing-backs high up the pitch and compress the space between the lines paid dividends, as City struggled to cope with the pressure.

"Conte got his tactics spot on in the second half," said former Tottenham player and pundit, Jermaine Jenas. "He freshened things up and gave City a different problem to solve. It was a great example of in-game management." (Source: BBC Sport)

The match also highlighted the importance of Kane, who has been instrumental in Tottenham's push for a top-four finish. The England captain has now scored 20 goals in the Premier League this season, and his hold-up play and link-up work were crucial in bringing others into the game.

Conclusion

The title race is well and truly alive, and the next few weeks will be crucial in determining the destination of the Premier League trophy. Tottenham's stunning draw with Manchester City has given Manchester United a glimmer of hope, and the Red Devils will be looking to capitalize on City's slip-up. As the season enters its final stretch, one thing is certain: it's going to be a thrilling ride to the finish line.
