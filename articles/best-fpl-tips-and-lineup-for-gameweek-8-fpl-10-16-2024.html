Top FPL Picks for Gameweek 8

As we head into Fantasy Premier League (FPL) Gameweek 8, it’s crucial to build a team that capitalizes on favorable matchups. Here are the top picks for each position:

Goalkeepers

Nick Pope (Newcastle, £5.5m): Newcastle faces Bournemouth, a team struggling in attack, making Pope a great option for clean sheet points.

Defenders

Kieran Trippier (Newcastle, £6.7m): His attacking returns, combined with a potential clean sheet against Bournemouth, make Trippier a must-have.

Pedro Porro (Tottenham, £5.0m): Facing Luton, Porro offers both defensive and attacking returns in this favorable fixture.

Midfielders

Mohamed Salah (Liverpool, £12.6m): Salah is in excellent form and faces a Brighton side that has struggled defensively, making him a strong captaincy option.

Son Heung-min (Tottenham, £9.3m): Playing centrally, Son has flourished, and against Luton, he’s expected to deliver big points.

Forwards

Erling Haaland (Man City, £14.1m): Despite a tough fixture against Arsenal, Haaland’s goal-scoring ability makes him hard to ignore.

Ollie Watkins (Aston Villa, £8.0m): Watkins is in fine form and faces Wolves, a team vulnerable at the back, making him a top pick this week.

Differential Picks

Raheem Sterling (Chelsea, £7.2m): Against Burnley, Sterling’s pace and creativity could bring returns for those looking for a low-ownership player.

Pedro Neto (Wolves, £5.7m): Neto has been consistent with assists and could perform well against Aston Villa.

Captaincy Choices

  1. Son Heung-min: With Luton’s weak defense, Son is a standout captain pick.
  2. Mohamed Salah: Brighton’s leaky defense makes Salah a strong contender for the armband.
  3. Erling Haaland: Despite the Arsenal fixture, Haaland’s ownership and goal potential cannot be overlooked.
