Arsenal 1-0 Crystal Palace: Eze Haunts Former Club as Gunners Stay Top

Match Overview and Key Moments

Arsenal maintained their grip on first place in the Premier League with a hard-fought 1-0 victory over Crystal Palace at the Emirates Stadium. The match's defining moment came courtesy of Eberechi Eze, who scored against his former employers in what proved to be the game's only goal.

The 25-year-old midfielder, who joined Arsenal from Crystal Palace in the summer transfer window, demonstrated no hesitation in celebrating after his acrobatic volley found the back of the net. The goal came from a set-piece situation, with Emi Buendía delivering a corner that was partially cleared before Eze pounced on the rebound with a spectacular right-footed finish from the center of the box.

Tactical Analysis

Mikel Arteta's side dominated possession throughout the match, controlling 56% of the ball while creating numerous chances. However, Crystal Palace's defensive organization under Oliver Glasner made it difficult for the Gunners to find their rhythm in the final third. The visitors, playing their characteristic 3-4-2-1 formation, looked dangerous on the counter-attack, with Jean-Philippe Mateta and Daniel Munoz causing problems for Arsenal's backline.

David Raya in the Arsenal goal made several important saves to preserve the clean sheet, particularly denying Munoz when he was released by Mateta in a promising position. The offside flag ultimately ruled out the effort, but it served as a reminder of Palace's threat on the break.

Implications for the Title Race

With this victory, Arsenal extended their unbeaten run to seven consecutive matches across all competitions and solidified their position at the top of the Premier League table with 22 points from nine games. The Gunners now sit four points clear of Manchester City after the champions' defeat to Aston Villa earlier in the day.

The win showcases Arsenal's newfound ability to grind out results even when not at their fluent best - a characteristic often associated with title-winning teams. Arteta's men have now conceded just three goals in nine league matches, demonstrating the defensive solidity that could prove crucial in their quest for a first Premier League title since 2004.

Player Performances

Eberechi Eze's goal against his former club was his first Premier League strike for Arsenal, and the midfielder's performance justified Arteta's faith in his £35 million summer signing. Declan Rice anchored the midfield effectively, while Bukayo Saka provided width and creativity down the right flank before being substituted in the 66th minute for Gabriel Martinelli.

For Crystal Palace, Dean Henderson in goal made several crucial saves to keep the scoreline respectable, while defender Marc Guehi marshalled a resilient defensive performance. Despite the defeat, Palace showed they remain a dangerous side, particularly with the creative talents of Adam Wharton and Daichi Kamada in midfield.

Aston Villa 1-0 Manchester City: Cash Strike Stuns Champions

The Upset of the Day

In what may prove to be one of the season's most significant results, Aston Villa defeated reigning champions Manchester City 1-0 at Villa Park. Matty Cash's stunning 19th-minute strike was enough to secure all three points for Unai Emery's side and hand Pep Guardiola's team only their second league defeat of the campaign.

Cash's Moment of Magic

The goal that decided the contest was a thing of beauty. Following a corner kick delivered by Emi Buendía, the ball fell to Lucas Digne on the left side. The French full-back's delivery picked out Cash on the edge of the penalty area, and the Polish international unleashed a powerful left-footed drive that flew into the bottom corner, leaving Manchester City goalkeeper Gianluigi Donnarumma with no chance.

The goal marked Villa's fourth from a set-piece this season, highlighting the meticulous work Emery's coaching staff dedicates to these crucial moments of the game.

Defensive Masterclass

What followed Cash's opener was a defensive clinic from Aston Villa. Emiliano Martínez in goal was imperious, making several world-class saves to deny City's attacking threats. The Argentine shot-stopper proved once again why he's considered among the Premier League's elite goalkeepers, pulling off a miraculous save late in the match when Villa came close to extending their lead.

Manchester City dominated possession with 52% of the ball and created several chances, but Villa's defensive structure remained resolute. Pau Torres and Ezri Konsa formed an impenetrable partnership at center-back, while Amadou Onana and Boubacar Kamara provided tireless protection in front of the defense.

City's Struggles Continue

For Manchester City, this defeat represents a concerning dip in form. Erling Haaland, who had been in sensational form earlier in the season with 11 goals, was kept quiet by Villa's organized defense. The Norwegian striker thought he had equalized late in the match, but his effort was correctly ruled out for offside, with the forward appearing to collide with the post in the process.

Guardiola's side struggled to break down Villa's compact defensive shape, with Phil Foden and Jeremy Doku unable to find the precision needed in the final third. The defeat leaves City four points behind Arsenal in the title race and raises questions about their ability to maintain their dominance in English football.

Villa's Resurgence

This victory was Aston Villa's fourth consecutive home win and their third triumph over Manchester City at Villa Park in recent years. Unai Emery has established Villa as one of the Premier League's most formidable teams at home, with their new approach combining defensive solidity with dangerous counter-attacking football.

The three points move Villa up to 15 points from nine games, keeping them firmly in the hunt for European qualification. Players like Ollie Watkins, John McGinn, and Morgan Rogers continue to impress under Emery's guidance, suggesting Villa could be genuine contenders for a top-six finish this season.

AFC Bournemouth 2-0 Nottingham Forest: Cherries Continue Impressive Start

Bournemouth's Best-Ever Premier League Beginning

AFC Bournemouth cruised to a comfortable 2-0 victory over struggling Nottingham Forest at Vitality Stadium, extending their remarkable start to the 2025-26 season. The Cherries now sit in fourth place with 18 points from nine matches - their best-ever start to a Premier League campaign.

Tavernier and Kroupi Strike

Marcus Tavernier opened the scoring in spectacular fashion in the 25th minute with a clever corner kick that caught everyone by surprise. The midfielder's delivery from the flag curled directly into the far post, with Nottingham Forest goalkeeper Matz Sels completely wrong-footed by the audacious effort. It was a moment of individual brilliance that showcased Tavernier's technical quality and game intelligence.

Bournemouth doubled their advantage just before halftime when teenage sensation Eli Kroupi struck in the 40th minute. The 19-year-old French forward, signed for £13 million from Lorient in the summer, has been a revelation for Andoni Iraola's side. This goal was Kroupi's fourth in his last three appearances, demonstrating remarkable efficiency with just 165 Premier League minutes under his belt.

Dominant Display

Bournemouth controlled proceedings throughout the match, registering 52.3% possession and creating 13 shot attempts compared to Forest's eight. Antoine Semenyo was particularly dangerous down the right wing, constantly troubling Forest's defense with his pace and direct running. The Ghanaian forward has been instrumental in Bournemouth's success this season, contributing nine goal involvements (six goals and three assists) in eight Premier League matches.

Forest's Nightmare Continue

For Nottingham Forest, this defeat represents their sixth of the season and their sixth match in which they've failed to score. The Tricky Trees remain rooted in 18th place with just five points from nine matches, sitting two points adrift of safety.

New manager Sean Dyche, who only took charge during the week after the dismissal of Ange Postecoglou, watched his team produce a dismal performance despite having overseen a 2-0 Europa League victory over Porto just days earlier. The contrast between Forest's European performance and their domestic struggles is stark and concerning.

Morgan Gibbs-White, Forest's creative talisman, was unable to unlock Bournemouth's well-organized defense, while striker Igor Jesus continues to search for his first Premier League goal of the season despite scoring five times in the Europa League.

Iraola's Tactical Triumph

Andoni Iraola's Bournemouth have been one of the Premier League's surprise packages this season. The Spanish coach has implemented an attractive, progressive style of football that has yielded impressive results. The Cherries' unbeaten run now stretches to seven league matches since their opening-day defeat to Liverpool, and they sit just four points behind leaders Arsenal.

Wolverhampton 2-3 Burnley: Clarets Snatch Late Winner in Thriller

Five-Goal Entertainment at Molineux

In what proved to be the afternoon's most entertaining encounter, Burnley secured a dramatic 3-2 victory at Molineux Stadium, inflicting further misery on rock-bottom Wolverhampton Wanderers. The match was a rollercoaster of emotions, featuring lead changes, comebacks, and a stoppage-time winner that sparked wild celebrations from the traveling supporters.

Flemming's Brace Puts Burnley in Control

Dutch midfielder Zian Flemming was the star of the show, scoring twice in the first half to give Burnley what seemed like a commanding advantage. The 26-year-old opened the scoring in the 14th minute with a right-footed finish after excellent build-up play involving Quilindschy Hartman. Just 16 minutes later, Flemming struck again, this time with his left foot, after Josh Cullen's clever through ball found Hartman, who provided the assist.

At 2-0 down, Wolves appeared destined for their ninth consecutive defeat across all competitions. However, the home side showed character and fighting spirit that had been absent in recent weeks.

Wolves' Fightback

Jørgen Strand Larsen pulled one back for Wolves in the 42nd minute from the penalty spot, giving the home fans hope heading into halftime. The Norwegian striker confidently converted after Burnley conceded a foul in the box, reducing the deficit at a crucial moment.

Just before the halftime whistle, in the 45th minute plus four seconds of added time, Wolves drew level through Marshall Munetsi. The Zimbabwean midfielder rose highest to meet a cross from Ladislav Krejčí with a powerful header that beat Burnley goalkeeper Martin Dúbravka, sending Molineux into raptures.

Foster's Late Heroics

The second half was a tense affair, with both teams creating chances but struggling to find the decisive goal. As the match entered its final moments, it appeared destined to end in a draw - a result that would have been welcomed by neither team given their respective positions in the table.

However, deep into stoppage time (90+5 minutes), Lyle Foster emerged as Burnley's hero. The South African striker, fed by Hannibal Mejbri, fired home with his left foot to secure a dramatic and vital three points for Scott Parker's side. The goal sparked jubilant scenes among the Burnley players and coaching staff, who recognized the significance of the victory in their battle against relegation.

Contrasting Fortunes

For Burnley, this victory lifts them to seven points from nine matches and out of the relegation zone, providing crucial breathing space in their fight for survival. The Clarets showed resilience and quality, particularly in the form of Flemming, who has now scored three goals in his last two Premier League appearances.

Wolverhampton Wanderers, conversely, remain anchored to the bottom of the table with just two points from nine matches. Vitor Pereira's side have now failed to win any of their first nine Premier League matches and face an increasingly desperate situation. The Wolves have scored only seven goals all season while conceding 19, highlighting problems at both ends of the pitch.

Everton vs Tottenham Hotspur: Late Kick-Off at Hill Dickinson Stadium

The final match of the Sunday schedule sees Everton host Tottenham Hotspur at the new Hill Dickinson Stadium (4:30 PM GMT / 12:30 PM ET). David Moyes's Everton side will be looking to maintain their impressive recent form at their new home, having won each of their last five Premier League matches played on Sundays.

Tottenham, under manager Thomas Frank, will be seeking to bounce back from their 2-1 defeat to Aston Villa in midweek. The North London side will be without several key players due to injury, including Cristian Romero, Dejan Kulusevski, and Dominic Solanke, making their task at a hostile away venue even more challenging.
