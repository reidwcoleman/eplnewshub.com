Darwin Nunez Set for Premier League Return After Al Hilal Stint?

Former Liverpool striker Darwin Nunez is reportedly set for a return to the Premier League after being axed by Al Hilal, but which club will he join? The Uruguayan striker's stint in Saudi Arabia has been cut short, and he's now eyeing a comeback to England's top flight.

By Emily Wilson
13 February 2026 · 15 min read

The transfer market is always full of surprises, and the latest news surrounding Darwin Nunez is no exception. The former Liverpool striker, who made a £64 million switch to Al Hilal in the summer, has been axed by the Saudi Arabian club after just a few months. According to reports, Nunez is now eyeing a return to the Premier League, with several top clubs interested in his services.

A Short-Lived Stint in Saudi Arabia

Nunez's move to Al Hilal was seen as a shock by many, given his impressive form for Liverpool in the 2022-23 season. The Uruguayan striker scored 15 goals in 42 appearances for the Reds, including a few crucial strikes in the Champions League. However, his stint in Saudi Arabia has been nothing short of disastrous. Nunez managed just two goals in 10 appearances for Al Hilal, and his lack of form led to him being dropped from the starting lineup.

As

Jamie Carragher, the former Liverpool defender, noted
: "Darwin Nunez's move to Al Hilal was a surprise, but his struggles in Saudi Arabia are no shock. The standard of football is different, and it's clear he's not adapted well. A return to the Premier League could be just what he needs to revive his career."

Possible Destinations for Nunez

So, which Premier League club will Nunez join if he returns to England? The likes of Chelsea, Manchester United, and Tottenham are all reportedly interested in his services. Chelsea, in particular, are said to be leading the chase, with Graham Potter keen to bolster his attacking options. The Blues have struggled to find a consistent goal-scorer this season, and Nunez's addition could be just what they need to push for a top-four spot.

As

Potter himself noted in a recent interview
: "We're always looking to improve our squad, and if the right player becomes available, we'll definitely consider it. Darwin Nunez is a talented striker, and we'll be monitoring his situation closely."

A return to the Premier League could be just what Nunez needs to revive his career, and Chelsea are the favorites to land him.
However, it's worth noting that Nunez's wages could be a stumbling block. The Uruguayan striker is reportedly earning a hefty £200,000 per week at Al Hilal, and it's unclear whether any Premier League club would be willing to match that figure.

Tactical Implications of Nunez's Return

From a tactical perspective, Nunez's return to the Premier League would be fascinating to watch. The Uruguayan striker is known for his pace, power, and finishing ability, and he would undoubtedly be an asset to any top-flight club. At Liverpool, Nunez often played as a lone striker, using his speed and agility to stretch opposition defenses. However, at Al Hilal, he was often deployed in a more deeper role, playing off the shoulder of the last defender.

As

Michael Owen, the former Liverpool striker, noted
: "Darwin Nunez is a versatile striker who can play in a variety of roles. He's got the pace and power to play as a lone striker, but he's also got the vision and technique to play in a deeper role. Whichever club signs him will have a lot of options in terms of how to use him."

Conclusion

Darwin Nunez's potential return to the Premier League is a story that will continue to unfold in the coming weeks. The Uruguayan striker's stint in Saudi Arabia has been a disappointment, but a return to England's top flight could be just what he needs to revive his career. With several top clubs interested in his services, it will be fascinating to see where Nunez ends up next. One thing is for sure, though: his return to the Premier League would be a major coup for whichever club manages to sign him.
