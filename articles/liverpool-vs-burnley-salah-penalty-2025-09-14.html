Liverpool's Late Heroics Continue in Premier League Clash

The Liverpool vs Burnley match on September 14, 2025, was a tense affair, with the Reds extending their flawless run to four consecutive wins, all clinched by goals in the final moments. Salah's 95th-minute penalty not only broke Burnley's stubborn resistance but also ended their 24-match home unbeaten streak, making this a pivotal moment in the Premier League 2025 season.

Key Insight: Liverpool become the first Premier League team to win four straight matches with goals in the final 10 minutes or later

Key Match Moments

First Half
Stalemate at Turf Moor
Liverpool dominated possession but struggled against Burnley's compact 5-4-1 setup. The Clarets' goalkeeper, Martin Dúbravka, was rarely tested, with Andy Robertson's shot being Liverpool's only effort on target.
Second Half
Intensity Ramps Up
Liverpool completed 94% of their 260 passes in Burnley's half and touched the ball 41 times in the opposition box. Dominik Szoboszlai and Ryan Gravenberch came close, but Burnley's defense blocked a staggering 12 shots.
85th Minute
Red Card Changes Everything
Burnley's Lesley Ugochukwu was sent off after a second yellow card, leaving the Clarets with 10 men and setting the stage for a dramatic finale.
95th Minute
Penalty Drama
A VAR-reviewed handball by Hannibal Mejbri handed Liverpool a penalty. Salah stepped up, slotting past Dúbravka to score his 188th Premier League goal.

Post-Match Analysis: Liverpool's Title Credentials Strengthened

This team has shown incredible mental strength. To win four games in this manner shows we have the character of champions.
Arne Slot, Liverpool Manager

Liverpool's knack for late winners is becoming a defining trait under Arne Slot. With 47 late winners in their history, Liverpool lead the competition by 13, showcasing their mental toughness and tactical flexibility.

However, the Reds' finishing remains a concern. Despite 26 shots, only three were on target before the penalty, suggesting a need for sharper attacking output. Florian Wirtz and Hugo Ekitike showed flashes of brilliance but lacked precision, an area Slot will likely address before their next clash against Manchester United.
